The first Saturday

March 2026

München has cycling groups. Plenty of them. Fast ones, social ones, ones that post more than they pedal. So why start another?

Ours came from a pretty simple observation. Most groups optimise for one thing. Speed, distance, content, clout. We wanted something that optimised for the actual experience of being on the bike with other people. The conversation on a quiet stretch of road south of the city. The stop that turns into an hour because the Biergarten is too good to leave. The feeling of rolling home tired and knowing it was a properly good day.

Smiles, not miles. That phrase gets thrown around a lot in cycling, usually by people selling something. For us it really does describe the filter we run every ride through. We care about where we go, who we go with, and whether the route has something worth finding along the way. We care less about average speed and weekly distance totals.

Exploring is a big part of it. München sits at the doorstep of some of the best riding in Europe, and most people barely scratch the surface. The Isar south towards Bad Tölz. Quiet roads through the Pfaffenwinkel. Gravel tracks into the foothills that feel like a different country. We spend time finding these routes so Saturday mornings feel like discoveries, even for people who have lived here for years.

Reliability matters to us too. When we say we ride on Saturday, we ride on Saturday. Through winter, through rain, through the months when the group shrinks to a handful. Consistency builds trust, and trust is what makes a group feel like something you can count on. You know the ride will happen. You know it will be well led. You know you will get home safe.

We ride two abreast because we actually want to talk to each other. There is space to push if you want it, and the regroup points mean nobody gets dropped. If you are new and feeling nervous about joining, that is completely normal. The only real expectations are that you are fit enough for the distance, your bike is in working order, and you come ready to be part of a group. Everything else you will pick up on the road.

The München cycling scene can feel like a lot of closed doors. Established groups with established dynamics, fast rides where you either keep up or disappear, communities that look welcoming online but feel hard to crack in person. We wanted Ours to be the opposite of that. Show up, say hello, ride your bike, have a coffee. Come back next week if you enjoyed it.

We are still figuring things out. The routes get better. The format evolves. But the reason for doing it stays the same. Good rides with good people, done with enough care that both of those things keep being true.
